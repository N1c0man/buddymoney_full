Snowball vs Avalanche: Best Way to Pay Off Debt Fast

Snowball vs avalanche—which debt payoff method is better? Learn the pros, cons, and which strategy will help you get out of debt faster.

Tue Mar 24 2026 17:00:00 GMT-0700 (Pacific Daylight Time) • 3 min read

If you’re trying to get out of debt, you’ve probably heard of two popular strategies:

  • The Snowball Method
  • The Avalanche Method

Both work—but the right one depends on your goals and personality.

What Is the Snowball Method?

The snowball method focuses on quick wins.

How it works:

  1. List your debts from smallest to largest
  2. Pay minimums on everything
  3. Put extra money toward the smallest debt first
  4. Once it’s paid off, roll that payment into the next debt

👉 Like a snowball, your progress builds momentum.

Why people love it

  • Fast psychological wins
  • Builds motivation
  • Easy to stick with

What Is the Avalanche Method?

The avalanche method focuses on saving money on interest.

How it works:

  1. List your debts from highest interest rate to lowest
  2. Pay minimums on everything
  3. Put extra money toward the highest interest debt first
  4. Move down the list as debts are eliminated

Why it’s powerful

  • Saves the most money long-term
  • Reduces total interest paid
  • Mathematically optimal

Snowball vs Avalanche: Key Differences

Snowball = Motivation first
Avalanche = Interest savings first

Feature Snowball Method Avalanche Method
Focus Small balances High interest rates
Motivation High Medium
Interest savings Lower Higher
Best for Staying consistent Saving money

Which Method Is Better?

👉 The honest answer: the one you’ll stick with

If you struggle with motivation:
→ Choose Snowball

If you want maximum efficiency:
→ Choose Avalanche

Real Example

Let’s say you have:

Credit Card A: $500 @ 18%
Credit Card B: $2,000 @ 22%
Loan: $5,000 @ 6%

Snowball:

Pay off $500 first → quick win

Avalanche:

Pay off 22% debt first → saves more interest

Want the Best of Both Worlds?

You can combine strategies:

  • Start with snowball for momentum
  • Switch to avalanche for efficiency

👉 This hybrid approach works very well in real life.
