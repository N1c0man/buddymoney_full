# What Credit Score Do You Need for a Secured Credit Card?

## What Credit Score Do You Need for a Secured Credit Card?

Most secured credit cards do not require a minimum credit score. You can qualify with no credit or a low score (300–600) because secured cards require a refundable deposit that reduces the lender’s risk.

---

If you're trying to rebuild your credit, one of the most common questions is:

👉 **“Do I need a certain credit score to get approved for a secured credit card?”**

The short answer:

> **Most secured credit cards do NOT require a minimum credit score.**

That’s exactly why they’re one of the easiest ways to start building credit.

## Why Secured Credit Cards Don’t Require High Credit Scores

Secured credit cards reduce risk for lenders by requiring a **refundable deposit**.

This deposit:

* Acts as your credit limit
* Protects the lender if you don’t pay
* Allows approval even with poor credit

---

## What Matters More Than Your Credit Score

Even though a high score isn’t required, approval still depends on a few factors:

### ✔ Your ability to fund the deposit

Most secured cards require **$200–$500** upfront.

### ✔ No recent major negative activity

Some issuers may still deny applications with:

* Recent bankruptcies
* Active collections in certain cases

### ✔ Basic identity and income verification

You still need to show you can manage payments.
