## Why You Need an Emergency Fund
* **Peace of mind:** Knowing you can handle unexpected costs reduces stress.  
* **Debt prevention:** Without a cushion, most people turn to credit cards or payday loans.  
* **Financial flexibility:** An emergency fund buys time to make smart decisions if your income changes.

Experts agree: a dedicated stash of cash is one of the smartest first steps toward financial independence.

## How Much Should You Save?
The classic rule of thumb is **three to six months of essential expenses.**  
That means rent or mortgage, utilities, food, insurance, and minimum debt payments.

* **Starter goal:** $1,000 for quick relief.  
* **Full goal:** 3â€“6 months of expenses based on your lifestyle and job stability.

## Where to Keep Your Emergency Fund
You want safety and easy access, not risky returns.

* **High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA):** FDIC-insured, earns interest, available in 1â€“2 days.  
* **Money Market Account:** Similar to HYSA with check-writing privileges.

Avoid locking this money into CDs or investments where withdrawals are difficult or could lose value.

## Common Questions
**Should I pay off debt or save first?**  
Aim for a small starter fund ($1,000) while making minimum debt payments.  
After that, aggressively pay high-interest debt and grow your emergency fund in parallel.

**What if I have irregular income?**  
Base your fund on **monthly essential costs** averaged over several months.  
Consider a higher target (six months) for extra security.
