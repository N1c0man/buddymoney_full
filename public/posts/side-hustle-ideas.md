# 7 Best Side Hustles You Can Start This Week (Even If You're Not Sure Where to Begin)

> TIP: Start with just one side hustle so you don’t burn out — consistency beats intensity.

Times are tough, and earning extra income isn’t just helpful… for many people, it’s necessary.  
The good news? You don’t need special skills, thousands of dollars, or months of planning.  
You need **something you can start fast** — and stay consistent with.

Below are the **7 best side hustles you can realistically start this week**, based on earning potential, flexibility, and startup cost.

---

## 1. Rideshare & Delivery Driving  
**Best for:** People with a car + flexible schedule  
**Eearning potential:** $18–$32/hr depending on city

Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, and Instacart are the fastest ways to begin earning immediately.

> NOTE: Track your mileage — it’s one of the biggest tax deductions independent drivers miss.

**Why it’s great:**  
- Paid quickly  
- You choose your hours  
- No boss  
- No learning curve

**BuddyMoney Tools to use:**  
- *Budget Tracker* → separate car expenses  
- *Emergency Fund Calculator* → save a portion of weekly earnings

---

## 2. Freelancing the Skills You Already Have  
**Best for:** Anyone with writing, design, editing, tutoring, coding, or admin skills  
**Earning potential:** $20–$75/hr

You don’t need to be an “expert” — beginners get hired every day.

Platforms to start:
- Upwork  
- Fiverr  
- Freelancer  
- LinkedIn Services

> TIP: Offer one simple service at first, like “Resume Cleanup” or “Blog Post Editing.”

---

## 3. Selling Items You Don’t Use  
**Best for:** Anyone with extra clothes, tech, or furniture  
**Earning potential:** $100–$500 in the first week

Apps like OfferUp, Facebook Marketplace, and eBay make this insanely easy.

Start with:
- Clothes you don’t wear  
- Old phones/laptops  
- Home decor  
- Small appliances  

Most people have $300–$1,000 of unused items sitting around.

---

## 4. Pet Sitting or Dog Walking  
**Best for:** Animal lovers  
**Earning potential:** $20–$60 per walk or visit

Rover and Wag can help you get started today.

**Why it works:**  
People love their pets. They will pay good money for reliable help.

> TIP: Offer photo updates — it boosts your reviews fast.

---

## 5. Flipping Items for Profit  
**Best for:** Treasure hunters & bargain-finders  
**Earning potential:** $25–$400 per flip

You can flip:
- Thrift store clothing  
- Furniture  
- Electronics  
- Tools  
- Collectibles  

Start small: buy one $5 item → sell it for $20.  
Repeat.

---

## 6. Remote Micro Jobs  
**Best for:** Anyone with a laptop  
**Earning potential:** $10–$25/hr

These tasks require **zero experience**:
- Data entry  
- Caption writing  
- Audio transcription  
- Simple research tasks  

Where to find them:
- Amazon MTurk  
- Clickworker  
- Appen  
- UserTesting  

They won’t make you rich — but they add up.

---

## 7. Create Digital Products  
**Best for:** People who want long-term, passive income  
**Earning potential:** Unlimited (seriously)

Sell:
- Budget templates  
- Printable trackers  
- Social media graphics  
- eBooks  
- Guided journals  

Platforms:
- Etsy  
- Gumroad  
- Shopify  

It takes work upfront, but once it’s created, income can become passive.

---

## Final Thoughts  
Side hustles are powerful because they create options.  
Extra income becomes:
- Savings  
- Emergency fund  
- Debt payoff  
- Future freedom  

If you’re unsure where to start, choose **whichever side hustle feels the least intimidating**.  
Start small, stay consistent, and build momentum.
